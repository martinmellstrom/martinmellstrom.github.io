§ 1

Overview

Audrig ("the App") is a music player for iOS developed by Martin Mellström, an individual developer based in Sweden. This Privacy Policy describes how the App handles your information.

The short version: Audrig does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to the developer. No account is required, and no tracking or analytics are used.

§ 2

Data We Do Not Collect

The developer does not collect any personal information from you. Specifically:

  • No name, email address, or other identifying information is collected.
  • No usage data, listening history, or behavioral data is collected or transmitted to the developer.
  • No crash reporting or analytics SDKs that transmit data to the developer are present in the App.
  • No advertising identifiers (IDFA) are accessed or used.
  • No advertising networks or third-party trackers are integrated.
  • No account registration is required to use the App.

The only network communication the App performs is directly between your device and Dropbox's servers, using your own Dropbox authorization.

§ 3

Dropbox Integration

Audrig connects to your personal Dropbox account using the official Dropbox API and OAuth 2.0 authorization. The following applies to this integration:

  • The App requests access to your Dropbox in order to allow you to browse folders and stream audio files you select. The App only reads folder structure and file content for playback purposes.
  • The only write operation the App performs is the one-time creation of an /AudRig folder in your Dropbox root, if it does not already exist.
  • Your Dropbox credentials are never transmitted to the developer. Authentication is handled entirely by Dropbox's own authorization flow.
  • An OAuth access token is stored locally on your device (in the iOS Keychain) solely to maintain your connection between sessions. This token never leaves your device to any server other than Dropbox's.
  • You can revoke the App's access to your Dropbox at any time via dropbox.com → Connected Apps. Revoking access removes the App's ability to read or write any data in your Dropbox.
  • Dropbox's own Privacy Policy governs how Dropbox handles your data. You can review it at dropbox.com/privacy.
§ 4

In-App Purchase and Apple

Audrig offers a one-time in-app purchase ("Premium Unlock") processed entirely by Apple through the App Store. The developer has no access to your payment information or Apple ID details.

  • The App uses Apple's StoreKit framework to verify purchase entitlements. This verification occurs between your device and Apple's servers only.
  • No purchase or transaction data is transmitted to or stored by the developer.
  • Apple's Privacy Policy governs data collected in connection with your App Store purchase. You can review it at apple.com/legal/privacy.
§ 5

Data Stored on Your Device

Some information is stored locally on your device to provide a functional experience. This data never leaves your device to the developer's servers:

  • Dropbox OAuth token — stored in the iOS Keychain to maintain your Dropbox connection between sessions.
  • Audio file cache — audio files you choose to make available offline are stored locally on your device. You can remove them at any time from within the App.
  • Waveform and metadata cache — waveform visualizations and file metadata are cached locally to improve performance. This data is derived solely from your own files.
  • Playback preferences — settings such as EQ, playback speed, and loop preferences are stored locally using iOS standard storage (UserDefaults).

All locally stored data can be removed by deleting the App from your device.

§ 6

Children's Privacy

Audrig is not directed at children under the age of 13. The App does not knowingly collect any personal information from children. If you believe a child under 13 has provided personal information through the App, please contact us so we can address the matter promptly.

§ 7

Third-Party Services

The App interacts with the following third-party services:

  • Dropbox — for accessing your audio files. Their privacy practices are governed by Dropbox's Privacy Policy.
  • Apple App Store / StoreKit — for processing in-app purchases. Their privacy practices are governed by Apple's Privacy Policy.

No other third-party services, SDKs, or APIs are used in the App.

§ 8

Your Rights (GDPR)

As the App is developed in Sweden and subject to the laws of the European Union, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) applies. Because the developer does not collect or process any personal data about you, there is no personal data held by the developer for you to access, correct, or request deletion of.

If you have questions about the data Dropbox or Apple holds about you, please refer to their respective privacy policies and contact them directly.

If you have any concerns about your privacy in relation to the App, please contact us using the details below.
