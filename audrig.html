The Idea

Built because nothing else fit

I'm a composer. My work lives in Dropbox - demos, mixes, stems, reference tracks, things I'm not done with. Dropbox's own player works, but it doesn't have the tools I needed. And every other app I tried was either too limited or built for something else entirely.

So I built the thing I wanted: a proper listening environment for your own audio, with the controls that actually matter for critical listening.

Audrig connects directly to your Dropbox, respects your folder structure, and stays out of the way.

Getting Started

Three steps

01

Connect Dropbox

Sign in with your Dropbox account when you first open the app. Audrig reads your library and any folders you add - nothing else is touched.

02

Add your files

Put your audio files in Dropbox at the path below. Organise with subfolders - each folder becomes a playlist. Tap + in the app to add more folders or shared folders from anywhere in your Dropbox.

Dropbox / AudRig /
03

Listen

Open the app and your library is there. Tap to play, download for offline, or open the professional listening tools. That's it.
