The Idea

Built because nothing else fit

I'm a composer. My work lives in Dropbox — demos, mixes, stems, reference tracks, things I'm not done with. To actually listen to them on the go, I had to forward files to myself, use apps that couldn't handle folders, or settle for whatever the system player offered.

None of it was right. A streaming service doesn't know about your personal files. A DAW is too heavy. The stock player has no tools. So I built the thing I wanted: a proper listening environment for your own audio, not someone else's library.

Audrig connects directly to your Dropbox, respects your folder structure, and gives you the kind of controls that matter for critical listening — without getting in the way.

Common Questions

FAQ

Do I need to pay? +

No — the free version is a fully functional music player. You can browse your Dropbox library, play files, use folders as playlists, download up to 10 files for offline use and control playback from the Lock Screen. Audrig All ($9.99, one-time) unlocks the professional listening tools: A/B Mode, Waveform, Freq Mode, Loop Mode, Mono Mode, Speed Control and unlimited offline sync.

Do I need Dropbox? +

Yes — version 1 of Audrig uses Dropbox as its cloud storage backend. A free Dropbox account is enough for most use cases. Google Drive support is planned for a future version.

What's the difference from a regular music player? +

Regular music players work with a streaming service or your local device library. Audrig is designed for your own audio files stored in Dropbox — demos, mixes, stems, recordings. On top of that, it includes professional listening tools like A/B comparison, frequency isolation and loop verification that no consumer music player offers.

What is A/B Mode? +

A/B Mode lets you load two audio files — say, two mixes of the same song — and switch between them instantly with zero latency. Both files are buffered in memory and play at the same position, so you hear the difference exactly where it matters. You can also run a blind test: shuffle the A/B assignment randomly and listen without knowing which is which, then reveal with an animation.

Is Audrig All a subscription? +

No. Audrig All is a one-time purchase. Pay once, own the features permanently. No recurring charges, no annual fees.
