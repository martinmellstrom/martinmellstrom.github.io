      The Composer

      Music that moves

      I make orchestral music for games. Smaller ensembles, a lot of minor keys, and enough ambient detail that a loop stays interesting after the twentieth time through.

      The goal is always that the music belongs in the scene. Not just underneath it. Wind in the right place, a texture that shifts as the tension builds, an instrument choice that fits the world.

      On the practical side: seamless loops that actually loop, multiple intensity layers you can trigger in engine, and stems for when you want to take it apart.

        Common Questions

        FAQ

        Can I use this music in my commercial indie game? +

        Yes — all my music packs are royalty-free and cleared for commercial use. You pay once and can use the music in your game indefinitely, across all platforms. No recurring licensing fees, no revenue splits, no attribution required.

        Do the tracks loop seamlessly? +

        Yes. Every track is designed as a seamless loop, integrating cleanly into Unity, Unreal, Godot or any other engine. The Atlas Companion pack includes battle layers for dynamic audio systems, and the Orchestral Exploration Pack includes tension layers for the same purpose.

        What game genres is the music suited for? +

        My packs work especially well for RPG, JRPG, adventure, Metroidvania, platformer and fantasy games — anything that benefits from an orchestral palette. The music is cinematic rather than chiptune, so it suits games with an immersive atmosphere.

        Is the music made by AI? +

        No. All my music is 100% human-composed. I write, arrange and produce every track using professional orchestral sample libraries alongside live instruments.

        Can I commission custom music for my game or film? +

        Yes — get in touch via the contact form below. I take on selected custom composition projects for games and film. Include a brief description of your project, genre and timeline and I'll get back to you within 1–2 days.
